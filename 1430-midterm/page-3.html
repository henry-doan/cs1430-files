Aye-Ayes

World's most unexpected creature

  • Name: Aye-Aye
  • Species Name:Daubentonia madagascariensis
  • Animal Kindom: Mammal
  • Diet-Type: Omnivores
  • Average-Life-Span: 20 years
  • Average Weight: 4-5lbs
  • Average Length: 16 in.
  • Native Origins: Madagascar

About:

The aye-aye is a dark fur, bushy long tail lemur found in Madagascar. It's key features are its long figures use for feeding, big eyes to see during the night, and strange ears to listen to their environment. The aye-ayes population is decreasing year by year which makes this primate endangered.

Diet:

  • Seeds
  • Fruits
  • Nectar
  • Fungus
  • Insects

The aye-ayes have long digits used as a tool for foraging. It uses its long finger to tap on branches and trunks as fast as 8 times per second and uses echolocation to find insects making aye-ayes the only primate so far to uses such method. The long digit is also used to fish out fruits and nectar.

Superstition

Many tribes native to Madagascar has a superstition of aye-ayes as a omen for bad luck. In a matter of fact that the natives are so superstitious of our furry little friends that they will kill aye-ayes if they encounter them. This action causes the almost extinction of the animals which today the animals and their habitats are protected by law.
